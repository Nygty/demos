Dear Nora and Franz, You have chosen the right destination — Fundão is genuinely the cherry capital of Portugal, with over 1.5 million cherry trees across the Cova da Beira valley, and the blossom is extraordinary. Typical peak: the last week of March to the first week of April, though this varies by up to 10 days depending on winter temperatures. The higher-altitude orchards (above 600m) bloom slightly later than the valley floor. We monitor conditions each year and can advise closer to your dates. Best spots: — The road between Alcongosta and Telhado (the "Rota da Cerejeira") is the classic route — a 15km arc through pure white blossom. — The Aldeia das Dez valley (45 min from Fundão): smaller, less visited, equally beautiful. — Alpedrinha village: the oldest cherry orchards in the region, with trees over 100 years old. The Festa da Cereja typically takes place in mid-June (cherry harvest, not blossom season) — but the blossom itself draws photographers from across Europe and no festival is needed. Could you share your planned travel year? I'll give you a more specific window based on the previous season's pattern. With pleasure, Concierge — Alambique de Ouro

Dear Peter and Alice, The Serra da Estrela is Portugal's highest mountain range and one of the most underrated landscapes in the country — your two days will be well spent. A suggested itinerary for moderate-level hikers: Day 1 — Vale do Zêzere: a classic glacial valley route from Manteigas (40 min from Fundão). 14km, 500m elevation gain, with the striking glacially-carved U-shape valley as your constant backdrop. Lunch stop at a shepherd's hut midway. Return to Alambique de Ouro for dinner and recovery. Day 2 — Torre plateau and Covão d'Ametade: the Torre (1,993m, highest point in mainland Portugal) accessible by vehicle, then a circular walk around the plateau and down to the glacial lake at Covão d'Ametade. 12km, 350m gain. Spectacular views in clear weather. Best season: May–June or September–October. July–August is fine but busy; winter (Nov–Mar) can bring snow at altitude. We work with Rui, a certified mountain guide based in Manteigas who speaks excellent English. His fee for 2 days: €180. Shall I check his availability for your dates? With enthusiasm, Concierge — Alambique de Ouro
