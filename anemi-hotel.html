Dear Tunde,

Chrysospilia is one of Folegandros' most remarkable secrets — you've done your research well! The cave is accessible only by boat, as the trail descent has been restricted in recent seasons.

The best way to experience it is a half-day boat excursion along the island's dramatic northern coastline: you moor outside the cave, swim inside (the light at midday is extraordinary), and continue to two or three other hidden coves. We can arrange this through our partner — typically a 4-hour morning trip with a local skipper.

For two hikers who love authenticity, we'd also recommend the trail from Chora down to Agios Nikolaos beach — wild, largely unmarked, and completely unspoiled. We'll put together a full day suggestion once you arrive.

Guest email received

Hi, we're planning to visit but we're struggling with the ferry connections from Athens. It seems like not many ferries go directly to Folegandros — can you advise on the best way to get there? We have flexibility on dates.

AI Draft — 30 seconds Ready to send

Dear Peter,

You're right that Folegandros is one of the less connected islands — and that's a large part of what keeps it so unspoiled! Here's our practical guide:

The most reliable route is the direct ferry from Piraeus (Athens port) with SeaJets or Blue Star Ferries — journey time is approximately 6–8 hours depending on the service, with crossings typically 2–3 times per week in summer. Alternatively, you can take a faster ferry to Santorini or Naxos and connect from there — often a shorter overall trip.

We recommend booking through Ferryhopper (ferryhopper.com) — it shows all available connections by date. Once you have your arrival ferry time, let us know and we'll arrange a transfer from the port of Karavostasi to the hotel.

Happy to help narrow down the best option once you have your travel dates confirmed.

Best regards,
The Concierge · Anemi Hotel, Folegandros

Dining in Chora Village
From: e.williamson@me.com
📧
Guest email received

Hi! Quick question — what are the best restaurants in Chora for dinner? We're looking for something with a view, ideally on the main square or the clifftop. We love good local food, not tourist menus. Any recommendations?

AI Draft — 30 seconds Ready to send

Dear Emma,

Great taste — Folegandros has a small but genuinely excellent dining scene, and Chora's main square in the evening is one of the loveliest places in the Cyclades.

For authentic local food with no tourist concessions, we'd particularly recommend Pounta on the main square — the grilled octopus and local cheeses are exceptional, and the tables spill out under the bougainvillea. Melissa nearby offers slightly more contemporary Greek cuisine with superb local ingredients.

For the clifftop experience, the terrace at Irini's has an uninterrupted view of the sea from the Chora promontory — best at sunset. During peak season, we recommend reserving a day ahead — we're happy to call on your behalf.

Bon appétit,
The Concierge · Anemi Hotel, Folegandros
