Dear Lars and Ingrid, Welcome to Thassos in advance — and don't worry, the logistics are straightforward once you know the route. From Thessaloniki airport: take a taxi or transfer (approx. 1h30) to Kavala Port — not the bus station, but specifically the Kavala Ferry Terminal at Prinos Pier (also called the "Thassos Line" terminal). In summer, ferries run approximately every 30–40 minutes from 06:00 to 22:00. The crossing to Skala Prinou (Thassos) takes approximately 35 minutes. From Skala Prinou ferry port to Archipelagos Resort: approx. 12km (20 minutes by car). We recommend arranging a taxi in advance from the port — taxis wait at the ferry dock but availability is inconsistent in peak summer. We can book a local taxi driver for you if you share your arrival ferry time. The driver can assist with heavy luggage. Alternatively, we offer a transfer service from Kavala airport for €80 per vehicle for up to 4 guests — this skips the ferry entirely and is door-to-door. Which option suits you better? Happy to arrange either. Warmly, Concierge — Archipelagos Resort

Dear Dr. Marsh, What a pleasure to welcome a classicist to Thassos — you have chosen one of the best-preserved ancient sites in the northern Aegean. The Theatre of Thassos (originally 3rd century BC, significantly restored in the Roman period) is set into the hillside above ancient Thassos (modern Limenas). The Kavala Cultural Society typically organises summer performances in July–August — ancient drama (usually Sophocles or Euripides) performed in the original theatre by Greek theatre companies. I will check the August programme and send you the specific dates this week. For the private guided visit: I can arrange a session with Elena, an archaeologist from the Thassos Archaeological Museum who has worked on the site for over a decade. Additional ancient sites worth your attention: — The Ancient Agora (adjacent to the theatre): well-preserved stoas and inscriptions — The Alyki Sanctuary of the Twin Temples: marble ruins descending into the sea — quite extraordinary — The Archaeological Museum in Limenas: the famous Kouros of Thassos (600 BC) is housed here Shall I contact Elena and begin arranging your schedule?
