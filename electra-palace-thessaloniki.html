Dear Jonathan and Philippa, October is an excellent time for this — the crowds have gone and the light inside the mosaic churches is exceptional at that time of year. Day 2 — Middle Byzantine Thessaloniki: Hagios Nikolaos Orfanos (14th-century frescoes considered among the finest in Greece), Hosios David (5th-century mosaic of Christ), and Hagios Panteleimon — typically ending with a private visit to the Byzantine Museum collections. Dear Sarah and Marco, You've heard correctly — and I say this as someone who works in this city every day: Thessaloniki's food culture is genuinely superior to Athens for everyday eating, and the street food in particular is in a completely different category. Honest recommendations: Street food and daytime: — Bougatsa: Grekas bakery (Egnatia Street, 5 min walk from the hotel) — a Thessaloniki institution since 1908. Custard or cheese filling, eaten standing at the counter at 08:30. — Koulouri Thessalonikis: from any street cart near the market — different from Athens, denser and sesame-rich. — Kapani Market (Modiano): Thessaloniki's covered market. Go Tuesday or Friday morning for the cheese and cured meat vendors. Excellent for grazing. For your special dinner: — Extravaganza (Modiano district): modern Macedonian cuisine, serious kitchen, no tourists. The lahanodolmades with smoked sheep's cheese and the sea bass carpaccio are exceptional. Book 2 days ahead. — Alternatively, Zythos Dore (near the White Tower) for a less formal but excellent taverna atmosphere.
