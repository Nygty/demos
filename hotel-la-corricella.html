Dear Annelise, This is exactly the right question to ask — and September is the ideal month, when the island returns to the locals after the summer crowds. Our three honest recommendations: — La Lampara (Via Roma): tiny, family-run, no menu on the wall — Signora Rosa tells you what she caught that morning. Book 24h ahead, strictly cash only. — Bar dal Cavaliere (Marina Corricella): not a restaurant but the best morning ritual on the island — coffee and freshly fried pizza fritta at the harbour at 7:30, alongside fishermen returning from the night. — Crescenzo (Marina Corricella): the one exception to "tourist" — owned by the Scotti family for three generations, and the linguine con l'aragosta is genuinely the best on the island. For food experiences: there is no formal cooking school on Procida, but we can arrange a private lesson with Nonna Carmela (a local grandmother who has been cooking for our hotel guests for years) on Tuesday mornings — fresh pasta, stuffed peppers and limoncello.
