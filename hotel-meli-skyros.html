Good morning. We came to Skyros specifically to see the Skyrian horses — we've read they're one of the rarest horse breeds in the world and can roam wild in the south of the island. Is it possible to arrange a guided horseback ride to see them? We'd also love to visit the Rupert Brooke memorial — we're both literature enthusiasts. And how do we get to the southern part of the island, is there transport or do we need a car?

AI Draft — ready in 30s ✓ Draft ready

Dear Alice and Robert,

What a wonderful reason to come to Skyros — and you're right, the Skyrian horse (Skyros pony) is one of the rarest horse breeds in the world, possibly descended from the ancient Greek horses depicted on the Parthenon friezes.

Horseback ride to see the Skyrian horses: We work with Skyrian Horse Farm (near Kalamitsa) — the dedicated conservation centre. They offer 2-hour guided rides into the southern plateau where the semi-wild herd grazes (€55/person). Suitable for beginners. It is the most moving experience Skyros offers — seeing these small, ancient horses completely free on the hillside above the sea. We book for you.

Rupert Brooke memorial: The grave and monument are in Tris Boukes bay in the far south — a moving, isolated spot. Accessible by boat (30 min from Linaria) or by car/ATV on a rough track. We recommend combining with the southern coast boat trip — see below.

Transport south: We rent ATVs through a trusted local partner (€35/day, no licence required) — the standard Skyros way to explore. Alternatively, we can arrange a private driver for a full-day island tour (€90). There is no public bus to the south.

We'll plan the full day for you,

Guten Tag Monika,

Skyros ist tatsächlich eine der letzten griechischen Inseln, auf der das traditionelle Kunsthandwerk noch aktiv und lebendig ist — eine Seltenheit.

Töpferei: Meisterin Nikos Papadimitriou in der Chora führt seit 30 Jahren eine kleine Töpferwerkstatt mit charakteristischen Skyros-Mustern (geometrische Muster in Blau, Weiß und Terrakotta). Workshopteilnahme auf Anfrage möglich (2 Stunden, ~€35). Wir stellen den Kontakt für Sie her.

Holzschnitzkunst: Skyros ist berühmt für seine handgeschnitzten Möbel und Truhen — besonders die Skyrian chests. Im Folklore-Museum der Chora (Manos Faltaits Museum) finden Sie die schönste Sammlung. Das Museum selbst ist außergewöhnlich gut kuratiert für eine so kleine Insel.

Chora-Rundgang: Planen Sie 2–3 Stunden ein: die kubistischen weißen Häuschen auf dem Fels sind einzigartig in Griechenland — erinnern an die Kykladen, aber wilder und weniger touristisch. Beginnen Sie am Kastro (oben) und schlendern Sie hinunter durch die engen Gassen. Am späten Nachmittag ist das Licht besonders schön.

Is there a flight option? We'll be flying into Athens first. Also — we're not hiring a car. Is it possible to get around Skyros without one? We prefer walking and cycling if that's viable.

AI Draft — ready in 30s ✓ Draft ready

Dear James and Diana,

Excellent questions — Skyros logistics are indeed a little more involved than other islands, which is part of why it remains so unspoiled.

Getting to Skyros:
Option 1 — Ferry from Kymi: Athens (Liossion bus terminal) → Kymi by bus (2.5 hrs, €14.50), then Skyros Shipping ferry Kymi → Linaria port (2 hrs, €12.50). Two daily departures in summer (usually 09:00 and 15:00). The crossing is comfortable and often spectacular — dolphins are common in August.
Option 2 — Sky Express flight: Athens → Skyros Airport is a 40-minute flight (€60–€120 one way, seasonal). We recommend booking 4–6 weeks ahead in August. The airport is 5 minutes from the hotel — we'll arrange the pickup.

Getting around without a car: Entirely possible for a relaxed stay. Skyros Town (Chora) is fully walkable — all tavernas, shops and the Kastro are on foot. For beaches, local buses run Chora → Magazia Beach (5 min, hourly in season, €1.50). For the south and more remote spots, we'd suggest one day with a rented ATV (€35) as the most enjoyable way to explore — not required but transformative.

Cycling: The north of the island has lovely quiet roads. We keep two bicycles available for guests at no charge — first come, first served.

Let us know your preferred arrival route and we'll help book everything.

Looking forward to welcoming you,
