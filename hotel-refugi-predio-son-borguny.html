Good morning, I'm a keen birdwatcher (RSPB member) and I'm particularly interested in visiting S'Albufera des Grau during my stay. Is there any specialist guide available? I'm hoping to see Egyptian vultures, black kites and Audouin's gulls. What's the best time of day?

AI Draft — 30 seconds Ready to send

Dear Richard,

S'Albufera des Grau is one of the finest wetland birding sites in the western Mediterranean — an excellent focus for your stay!

For the species you've mentioned: Egyptian vultures are most reliably seen in the gorge areas further inland (the Barranc de Binigaus near Sant Tomàs is excellent), while Audouin's gulls congregate on the islets near S'Albufera's lagoon — dawn visits on calm mornings are the most productive. Black kites tend to be visible over the farmland between Ferreries and Alaior from mid-morning.

We can put you in contact with a local ornithologist who guides small groups in the reserve — he knows the exact spots for each species and can advise on what's present during your specific dates. Would a half-day guided visit interest you?

Best regards,
The Concierge · Son Borguny, Menorca
