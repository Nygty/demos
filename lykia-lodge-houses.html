Snorkelling & Blue Cave Exploration
From: fiona.mclachlan@sky.com
📧
Guest email received

Hi there, we're keen snorkellers and would love to find some really clear water with good marine life near Kalkan. Are there organised snorkelling trips, or is it better to hire a kayak and go independently? We've read about a blue cave somewhere?

AI Draft — 30 seconds Ready to send

Dear Fiona,

You're in the right place — the waters around Kalkan are among the clearest on the Turkish coast, with excellent visibility and diverse marine life.

The Blue Cave you've read about is Mavi Mağara, accessible by kayak or small boat about 2 km along the coast. It's best visited in the morning when the light streams in and creates the famous blue glow inside. For snorkellers, the sea grass meadows and rocky outcrops around Kaputaş Gorge (20 minutes west) are superb — you'll encounter octopus, sea bream, moray eels and, in season, the odd sea turtle.

We'd suggest a half-day kayak rental from the harbour (we have a trusted local supplier) with a waterproof map of the best spots — completely independent and very rewarding. Alternatively, there are small-boat snorkelling day trips that cover both the cave and Kaputaş. Which would you prefer?

Warmly,
The Concierge · Lykia Lodge, Kalkan
