Guest · The Nakamura Family
We will be staying for 4 nights with our two children (ages 8 and 11). What activities would you suggest in and around Saint-Rémy-de-Provence? We love history, nature and good food!
AI Concierge Reply drafted in 31s

Dear Nakamura family,

Four nights in Provence with a love of history, nature and food — you have come to exactly the right place! Here are our warmest recommendations for all ages:

History & Culture: The Roman ruins of Glanum are a ten-minute walk from the Mas — your children will love exploring the ancient city walls and aqueduct. The nearby village of Les Baux-de-Provence perched dramatically on a cliff is unforgettable, and the Carrières de Lumières immersive art show within the limestone caves is truly magical for young visitors.

Nature: A guided walk or cycle through the Alpilles Regional Park winds through olive groves, lavender fields and wild garrigue — we can arrange bikes and a guide directly from the estate. For a gentle half-day, the old market town of Saint-Rémy on Wednesday morning is a feast of local colour and produce.

Food: We would love to arrange a private Provençal cooking class with our chef for the family — a wonderful afternoon making tapenade, socca and tian de légumes together before dinner.

We will prepare a personalised day-by-day programme and leave it in your suite on arrival.
