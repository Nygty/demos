Dear Morrison family,

What a wonderful day you have planned — Carmona to Seville is one of the great Andalusian excursions, and at just 35km it could not be easier. Here is everything you need:

1. Transport to Seville: The simplest option is a private taxi from the Parador (€35 one-way, 35 minutes, we arrange on your behalf). For independence during the day, the DAMAS bus service runs from Carmona bus station (a 5-minute taxi from us) to Seville Prado de San Sebastián, every hour, €3.70/person — a great budget option.

4. Suggested departure: Leave the Parador at 08:30 — the Alcázar opens at 09:30 and the morning light in the royal gardens is exceptional. This gives you the cathedral by midday, lunch in the barrio, and a leisurely afternoon before returning by 18:00 for dinner on our fortress terrace.

Dear Professor Aldred,

We are delighted to welcome you — the Roman necropolis of Carmona is one of the most important funerary sites of Roman Hispania, and the Parador itself stands on the foundations of the ancient fortress. You are, in a very real sense, staying inside history.

1. Necropolis opening hours: The Necrópolis Romana de Carmona is open Tuesday–Sunday, 09:00–17:00 (summer schedule). Entry is free for EU citizens with academic credentials (your university ID qualifies). It is a 10-minute walk from the Parador.
