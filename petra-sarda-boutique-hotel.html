Hello, we're staying with you from 20th July. We'd love to visit Neptune's Grotto — is it better to take the boat from the port in Alghero or drive to Capo Caccia? Any advice on the best experience? There are four of us including two children (8 and 11).

AI Draft — 30 seconds Ready to send

Dear James,

Neptune's Grotto is absolutely spectacular — a great choice for the family! Here's our honest advice on the two options:

For families with children, the boat from Alghero port is usually the favourite — the 45-minute crossing along the dramatic Capo Caccia cliffs is memorable in itself, and at 8 and 11 years old your children will love it. It departs from the harbour (a 10-minute walk from the hotel) and runs several times daily in summer. At the grotto, the guided visit takes about 45 minutes inside the cave system.

The Capo Caccia road option involves a steep descent (654 steps — the Escala del Cabirol) which can be tiring on the return. We'd suggest the boat for this visit, and save Capo Caccia for a scenic drive another day.

Shall we reserve the morning boat for the 21st? It tends to be less crowded than the afternoon.
