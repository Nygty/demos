Dear John and Patricia, What a pleasure to welcome two historians — you will feel a deep connection with the story of this extraordinary place. The Mosteiro de Santa Maria de Bouro was founded in the 12th century by Cistercian monks, part of the great monastic reform movement that spread from Cîteaux in Burgundy across all of Christian Europe. For over 600 years it was a centre of learning, agriculture and spiritual life in the Minho — until the dissolution of the monasteries in 1834, when it fell silent for 150 years. The building you will sleep in was then entrusted to the architect Eduardo Souto de Moura, who won the Pritzker Prize for his thoughtful conversion — preserving every medieval stone while creating spaces of extraordinary serenity. For a guided tour of the Romanesque cloister, the refectory with its 17th-century tilework and the granite façade, we work with Dr. Ana Lima, a local historian who leads private visits for guests with a deeper interest.
