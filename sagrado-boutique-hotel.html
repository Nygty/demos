Dear Andrew and Helen, You have chosen a wonderful time to visit — September in the Ria Formosa is genuinely exceptional for birdwatching. Flamingos, spoonbills, Kentish plovers and early waders from northern Europe begin arriving in significant numbers, and the lagoon light in autumn is magnificent. I can arrange a full day combining both experiences: Morning (08:30 departure): A 2-hour guided birdwatching boat tour of the Ria Formosa lagoon with João, a certified ornithologist and naturalist guide. Afternoon (12:00): The boat continues to Ilha Deserta — the most remote and pristine of the barrier islands, accessible only by boat. Dear Jan, Welcome — Faro's walled old town (Cidade Velha) is one of the most underrated historic centres in all of Portugal, and a pleasure to explore on foot. For the walking tour, I can arrange a private 2-hour guided walk with Sofia, a local art historian who specialises in the history of the Islamic Algarve and the post-Reconquista architecture. The route includes: the original Moorish walls and Arco da Vila (the 18th-century gateway built over the original Islamic gate), the Sé Cathedral (begun in the 13th century on the site of a mosque), the bone chapel of Nossa Senhora do Carmo, and the quiet Largo da Sé square. This typically takes place in the late afternoon when the light is beautiful on the old stone — 17:00 departure from Sagrado. For dinner on October 5th, I recommend Restaurante Dois Irmãos, a Faro institution since 1925, just 3 minutes from the hotel — excellent cataplana (traditional Algarvian clay pot) of clams and pork, outstanding tuna à lagareiro with olive oil, fair prices. I can make a reservation for you at 20:00. Shall I confirm both? With pleasure, Sagrado Boutique Hotel — Faro
